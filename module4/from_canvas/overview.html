[Computational Economics]

 

Overview

In this module, we delve into the Solow-Swan growth model, a foundational concept in understanding economic growth. You will learn to articulate the mechanisms of this model, including its key assumptions and how it explains the process of economic development. The module emphasizes differentiating between catch-up growth, where economies grow by adopting existing technologies, and cutting-edge growth, characterized by innovation-driven expansion. A significant portion of the course involves practical application: you'll use Python to simulate the Solow-Swan model, providing a dynamic way to visualize and comprehend economic growth under varying scenarios. These simulations will also be instrumental in exploring how changes in model parameters, like savings rates or technological advancement, can impact growth. This comprehensive approach offers a blend of theoretical insights and practical skills, equipping you with a deeper understanding of economic growth dynamics.

Learning Objectives

At the end of this week you should be able to: 

  1. carefully explain the Solow-Swan growth model
  2. use the Solow-Swan growth model to explain economic growth
  3. explain the difference between catch-up growth and cutting edge growth
  4. use python simulate the economic growth
  5. use simulation methods to test how the changes in model parameters may impact growth
